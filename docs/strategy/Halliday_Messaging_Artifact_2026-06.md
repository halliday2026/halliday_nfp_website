# Halliday — Core Messaging Artifact
**Thread 1 (the trunk) · Locked June 2026**

> Canonical messaging. This is the trunk artifact — the service architecture (Thread 2), gateway calculator (Thread 3), content/outreach (Thread 4), and the website rebuild all inherit this language. It supersedes the prior site copy, the four capability pillars ("data collection automation / analysis / reporting-viz / custom tooling"), and the "$2M–$20M budgets" framing in the Portfolio Overview.

---

## Hero

**Halliday builds nonprofits a professional web presence — and the custom data tools to raise more, report faster, and prove their impact.**

Why this line:
- Leads with the **fast-yes** (the website program — the offer that closes without waiting on a grant cycle), so the strongest near-term cash offer is in plain sight, not buried.
- The **data tools carry the three outcomes** (raise more / report faster / prove impact) and the premium. The seam in earlier drafts — where "report faster" was awkwardly attached to a website — is fixed: those three outcomes clearly belong to the data work.
- "Custom data tools" names the moat (the domain that justifies the price), not "apps" (reads as generic software) and not "custom build" or "AI" (the HOW, never the flag).
- No price, no "fast," no "affordable."

## The funnel in one line (wedge → flag)

Sits directly under the hero:

**Start with a professional website we build and run for you — then grow into the data tools as you're ready.**

This makes the website→data path legible from the first screen: the website is the front door, stated as the front door, and the data work is the room you grow into.

## Phasing rule (important — read before re-weighting the hero)

Lead with the synthesis hero **now**. Website MRR is the offer that closes fast; the data work is gated behind grant cycles, so leading with the open door is honest and accelerative.

**Tighten the hero onto data when Gate 1 goes green** — i.e., once the productized fixed-scope data offer has sold 3+ times at the $7,500+ floor. Tie the shift to that trigger, not to a vibe, or "pivot later" quietly becomes "never" (website cash will always feel safer than the data climb).

**Phase the emphasis, never the identity.** Halliday is — and remains — a data consultancy with websites as the front door. The destination is fixed; only the hero's emphasis tracks the proof. This is a tightening, not a pivot. Treating it as a real business-model change (web-shop brand now, data brand later) makes you pay the repositioning tax twice — every website client filed under "web company" has to be re-taught you're a data firm. The synthesis hero avoids this by keeping data visible from day one.

## WHO

**Who has the problem (data work — the expansion target):** nonprofits in the *messy middle*, roughly **$500K–$2M** budget. Big enough to run several disconnected systems and carry a real grant-reporting load; too lean to afford a data hire or a well-implemented enterprise CRM.

**Acquisition is wider than expansion.** The website wedge is **size-agnostic** — any nonprofit (and adjacent professionals) needing a credible web presence. Go wide for acquisition; target the messy middle for the data-work upsell.

**Chase visible signals over budget figures:**
- Data living in 3+ places
- A recent grant win (new money *and* new reporting obligations)
- No in-house data person
- Board decks assembled by hand

**Decision-maker:**
- **Executive Director** — primary. Owns the vendor call at this size; feels the board-and-funder reporting pain personally.
- **Development Director** — secondary champion. Feels the donor-data pain most acutely.
- **Board** — usually the *pressure source* ("we need better numbers"), rarely the buyer.

**Kill this:** the Portfolio Overview line stating Halliday serves *"$2M–$20M budgets."* It directly contradicts the June targeting decision and is almost certainly why Amber read the site as "for large orgs." Replace with the messy-middle framing above.

## Before / After (the data-tier payoff)

This is the emotional core of the data work — Amber's framing.

**Before:** Data in five places. Staff burning hours stitching spreadsheets together. A major donor lapses and nobody notices until the year-end numbers come in. Board reports take a week and still don't tell the story. Impact you can feel but can't prove to a funder.

**After:** One connected picture. Hours back. Lapsing donors flagged while you can still act. Reports in minutes. Impact you can show a funder on demand.

## Pillars

One entry pillar (the wedge) + three data pillars that rhyme with the hero's three outcomes. "Custom build" appears only inside Pillar 4 — the HOW, discoverable in the body, never the flag.

**Start here — A web presence that's handled for you.**
A professional website we build and keep running for you. A real person makes sure it's always current and working, so you never have to think about it again. (This is where most clients begin — and the door into the data work.)
*Register: done-for-you + peace of mind. Premium-of-care, not cheap. Never "easy/fast/affordable" as the loud note.*

**Raise more — See what your donor data's been hiding.**
Know which donors are slipping *before* they're gone, who your major supporters really are, and where retention is leaking — while you can still act, not in next year's post-mortem.

**Report faster — Walk into the board meeting ready.**
Grant reports and board decks that assemble themselves from the numbers across all your systems. The week-long scramble becomes minutes.

**Prove impact — Show your work, on demand.**
The tool built to match how your programs actually run — turning the impact you can feel into the impact you can show a funder. When nothing off the shelf fits, you get the one that does.

Adjacent pros (Donna, Klara, etc.) can buy the website program *and* serve as a distribution channel into nonprofits. They are a channel, not a separate market. Breadth lives here in the body; the hero stays nonprofit-specific.

## Operating rules (carry into every downstream artifact)

1. **Lead with the customer + outcome.** Never lead with "custom build" or "AI."
2. **Custom build is the HOW** — discoverable in the body (Pillar 4), never the flag.
3. **Deliver with AI, never sell the AI.** It's the delivery mechanism, not the marketing message.
4. **Never market "cheaper" externally.** AI efficiency is margin, not a client discount. Watch the three quiet doors cheapness sneaks through:
   - a **price tag in positioning** ("$75/mo" anywhere near the hero anchors the whole brand on the cheapest thing you sell — price lives on a pricing page);
   - **"easy / simple / no-hassle" as the *loudest* note** (true and fine, but if it dominates it reads as low-value);
   - **speed-as-the-pitch** ("fast" leads → sounds like cheap's cousin; mention it, never fly it).
5. **Website value prop = a real person + peace of mind** (done-for-you), not hosting infrastructure and not price.
6. **Hero flies the wedge now, tightens onto data at Gate 1.** Emphasis phases; identity doesn't.
