## The two-tier map

**Flag — Nonprofit data consulting.**
Narrow, high-value, $7,500+ floor. Gated behind grant cycles and trust — *not* an impulse buy. This is the identity and the margin. Maps to the hero's three outcomes (raise more / report faster / prove impact).

**Wedge — Managed Websites.**
$75/mo, size-agnostic, fast-yes, recurring revenue. Closes without a grant cycle. Does three jobs: smooths cash flow, manufactures a trusted already-paying monthly relationship, and opens the upsell door.

**The connective tissue (don't leave this implicit):**
The website isn't just a cheap sale — it's a **standing relationship with a built-in reason for monthly contact.** The "real person who keeps your site running" is also the person who's around when the ED mentions the board deck took all week. That recurring touch *is* the upsell surface. Without it, the wedge is low-margin web work; with it, it's lead-gen that pays for itself.

## Path 1 — Wedge → data upsell

**Trigger (what you listen for inside an existing website relationship):**
- A recent grant win (new money *and* new reporting obligations)
- "Our data's in five places"
- Board decks assembled by hand
- No in-house data person

Because you're already in the account monthly, these surface organically — you hear them instead of cold-prospecting for them.

**The offer they step into:** the single productized data engagement below (not a custom proposal written from scratch each time). The upsell is "here's the obvious next step," not a fresh sales cycle.

## Path 2 — Adjacent-pro referral

Donna and Klara are *both* website clients and channels — that's the elegant part. The path:

great done-for-you website experience → they trust Halliday → their nonprofit clients (the messy-middle targets) need data help → they refer.

To keep this real and not hopeful, three mechanics:
1. **Log them in Folk** as referral-partner with a follow-up cadence.
2. **Give them a dead-simple warm-intro path** — a one-paragraph "here's who I'd help and how" they can forward.
3. **Keep them visible to the work** via the LinkedIn motion (Thread 4), so referring feels natural.

They are a **channel, never a separate market.** Breadth stays in the body, never the hero.

## The productized data offer (Gate 1)

This is the cure for bespoke-every-time scoping *and* the first expansion gate.

**One fixed-scope, fixed-price data engagement, sold the same way every time.**

**The offer (provisional name: Donor Insight Engagement — name not yet locked):**
The EHL-style donor analysis, delivered done-for-you: donor retention, lapsing/at-risk identification, lifecycle segmentation, and top-donor trends — with the insights and the recommended actions, not just a data dump.

**Why this one:**
- Repeatable and already proven (EHL pilot).
- Maps exactly onto the "raise more" pillar.
- It's the human-delivered version of what Signal would later automate — which keeps **Signal a downstream product experiment, not a prerequisite.** You can sell this engagement by hand today; Signal productizes it later, only if validated (see Thread 1 validate-before-build discipline).

**Price: $7,500 fixed (the floor).** No discounting, no tiered basic/pro options that reopen the price downward.

**Gate 1 is green when this offer has sold the same way 3+ times at the $7,500+ floor.** That trigger is also what re-weights the hero onto data (Thread 1 phasing rule).

## Pricing reconciliation (resolves the Thread 1 open item)

**Kill the $2,500 "Impact Data Review" line in the Portfolio Overview.** The productized data offer anchors at the **$7,500 floor.** No sub-floor paid diagnostic.

Rationale:
- The jump from $75/mo to $7,500 *feels* big, but the bridge is **not** a cheap paid diagnostic that re-anchors the buyer low. The bridge is the **gateway calculator (Thread 3) + a free discovery conversation** doing the qualifying.
- A $2,500 "review" doesn't bridge the gap — it teaches the buyer the data work is cheap and makes the eventual $7,500 ask feel like a price hike instead of the real number.
- **One cheap door is sufficient.** The website relationship already provides the low-friction front door; a second cheap door does more harm than good given the standing undercharging tendency.
